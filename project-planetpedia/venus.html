Venus


Venus is similar in size to Earth, with a diameter of about 12,104 kilometers (7,521 miles), making it the sixth-largest planet in the solar system. Its mass is approximately 86% of Earth's mass.Venus has a thick and extremely dense atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide (about 96.5%) with clouds of sulfuric acid. This thick atmosphere traps heat, leading to a runaway greenhouse effect and extremely high temperatures on the surface.Venus has one of the most inhospitable environments in the solar system, with surface temperatures that can soar to around 900 degrees Fahrenheit (475 degrees Celsius), hotter than the surface of Mercury, despite being farther from the Sun.
